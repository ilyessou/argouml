15.1. Introduction

This chapter describes each artifact that can be created within a use case diagram. Note that some sub-artifacts of artifacts on the diagram may not actually themselves appear on the diagram.

There is a close relationship between this material and the properties tab of the details pane (see Section 10.4). That section covers properties in general, in this chapter they are linked to specific artifacts.

15.1.1. ArgoUML Limitations Concerning Use Case Diagrams

During the standardization process of UML, there was considerable change over the definition of Includes (originally known as Uses) and Extends. Initially they were specializations of Generalization within the UML metamodel (itself a specialization of Relationship.

In UML 1.3, Includes and Extends are defined as specializations directly from Relationship.

Warning

ArgoUML further confuses matters by defining Includes and Extends as stereotyped specializations of Dependency (itself a specialization of Relationship. This is an area where ArgoUML is out of line at present with the standard.

Further difficulties arise with Extension Points, the points within a use case, where extending use cases may be invoked. In the absence of a LocationReference within ArgoUML these are restricted to purely documentation features.
